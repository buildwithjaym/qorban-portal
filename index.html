The problem

When grading depends on paper and spreadsheets, the process becomes slower, heavier, and harder to manage.

The challenge is not only about technology. It is about time, accuracy, visibility, and operational clarity. If teachers must repeat work across paper records and Excel files, the grading process becomes more difficult than it needs to be.

01

Too much repeated work

Teachers may record grades manually first, then transfer them again to another format. This consumes time and adds unnecessary effort every grading period.

02

Limited student visibility

Students often wait until much later to know their academic standing, instead of seeing their grades as soon as they are available.

03

Harder admin follow-up

Without a centralized portal, administrators may spend more effort checking who has submitted grades and which classes still need attention.

The solution

A focused portal designed around the real workflow of QITTACI.

The first version of this portal is intentionally simple. It focuses on one high-value workflow: teachers upload grades, students view them directly, and administrators monitor progress.

By keeping the MVP focused, the school gets a practical system that solves a real operational problem without adding unnecessary complexity.

Teacher

Upload grades with less friction

Teachers access only their assigned subjects and sections, making grade entry cleaner, faster, and more secure.

Student

See grades with more transparency

Students can log in and directly view their uploaded grades, giving them better awareness of their academic standing.

Admin

Monitor the process with clarity

Administrators gain a clear view of submission progress without interfering with teacher-entered grade records.

What QITTACI gains from this portal

How it works

A simple workflow that matches the MVP

The portal is designed to be easy to understand, easy to use, and aligned with the school’s immediate needs.

1

Teacher logs in

The teacher sees only the subjects and sections assigned to them.

2

Teacher uploads grades

Grades are entered directly into the portal instead of relying only on manual methods.

3

Students view grades

Students can access their uploaded grades directly from their account.

4

Admin monitors submissions

School administration can track overall submission activity and workflow progress.

Required datasets

Data needed to initialize the portal for QITTACI

To launch the portal correctly, the school only needs a small set of core records. These datasets will allow the system to organize users, subjects, assignments, and grades accurately.

Student list

Student ID, full name, grade level, and section.

Teacher list

Teacher ID, full name, and basic account details.

Subjects offered

The complete list of subjects handled by the school.

Sections and classes

Grade level and section structure for enrolled students.

Teacher assignments

Which teacher handles which subject and section.

Grading periods

The grading structure used by the school, such as quarters or terms.
