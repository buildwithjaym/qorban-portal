QITTACI Portal Vision

What we want to improve

  • Manual recording of grades on paper
  • Repeated transfer of records into Excel
  • Delayed release of grades to students
  • Limited admin visibility on submissions

What the portal will do

  • Teachers upload grades directly
  • Students view grades in one portal
  • Admins monitor submission status
  • School workflow becomes more organized
Current Situation

The current grading process can become slow, repetitive, and difficult to monitor

If the current workflow relies on paper records and Excel files, teachers spend extra time encoding, students wait longer to see results, and administrators have fewer tools to monitor the process.

01

Repeated Teacher Work

Teachers may record grades manually first, then re-encode them again in another format, which adds unnecessary time and effort.

02

Delayed Student Access

Students often only learn their standing later, instead of having timely visibility into their academic performance.

03

Limited Monitoring

Admins may find it harder to track who has already submitted grades and which classes still need follow-up.

Proposed Solution

A focused grade portal built around the actual needs of QITTACI

The MVP is intentionally simple. It focuses on one important school need: teachers upload grades, students view them directly, and admins monitor the process.

Teacher

Grade Uploading

Teachers will only access their assigned subjects and sections, making grade submission cleaner and more secure.

Student

Grade Visibility

Students can log in and directly view uploaded grades, giving them better awareness of their academic progress.

Admin

Submission Monitoring

Admins can monitor grade submissions and portal activity without changing teacher-submitted grades.

Why It Matters

How this portal can help QITTACI

More efficient grading workflow

Reduce unnecessary repetition by giving teachers one place to submit grades.

More transparent academic records

Students can access grades more directly instead of waiting only for end-period release.

Better administrative oversight

The school can better monitor grade submission progress and encourage timely completion.

Supports digital transformation

This is a practical first step toward a more organized and modern school process.

Workflow

How the proposed portal will work

1. Teacher logs in

Each teacher accesses only the subjects and sections assigned to them.

2. Teacher uploads grades

Grades are entered directly into the portal instead of depending only on manual processes.

3. Students view grades

Students can check their grades directly after the teacher uploads them.

4. Admin monitors progress

Admins can see which submissions are done and which require follow-up.

Proposal Summary

A practical portal proposal for QITTACI

This portal is designed not to add complexity, but to solve a real operational problem: making grade submission simpler, grade viewing more transparent, and monitoring easier for the school.
